## Part I: Cl(6) Construction from the Octonion Splitting O = C + C^3

### Step 1: The Octonion Splitting

Choose a unit imaginary octonion $u \in S^6 \subset \mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{O})$. Following the Fano convention, we set $u = e_7$.

This defines an embedding of $\mathbb{C}$ into $\mathbb{O}$:
$$\mathbb{C} = \mathrm{span}_{\mathbb{R}}\{1, e_7\} \subset \mathbb{O}$$

The orthogonal complement of $u$ in $\mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{O})$ is 6-dimensional:
$$\mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{O}) = \mathrm{span}\{e_7\} \oplus W, \qquad W = e_7^{\perp} \cap \mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{O}), \quad \dim(W) = 6$$

Explicitly:
$$W = \mathrm{span}_{\mathbb{R}}\{e_1, e_2, e_3, e_4, e_5, e_6\}$$

The space $W$ inherits a complex structure from left multiplication by $u$:
$$J: W \to W, \qquad J(w) = e_7 \cdot w$$

To verify $J^2 = -\mathrm{Id}$: for any $w \in W$, by the alternativity of $\mathbb{O}$:
$$J(J(w)) = e_7 \cdot (e_7 \cdot w) = (e_7 \cdot e_7) \cdot w = -w$$
where the second equality uses the alternative law $x \cdot (x \cdot y) = (x \cdot x) \cdot y$ (left alternative identity), valid in any alternative algebra, and $e_7^2 = -1$.

**Explicit check on basis element:** $J(e_1) = e_7 \cdot e_1$. From the Fano convention with $e_1 e_2 = e_4$, the multiplication table gives $e_7 e_1 = e_3$ (this follows from the Fano plane relations). Then:
$$J(J(e_1)) = e_7 \cdot (e_7 \cdot e_1) = e_7 \cdot e_3 = -e_1$$
confirming $J^2 = -\mathrm{Id}$ on this basis element.

*(Note: The specific products $e_7 e_k$ depend on the Fano plane labeling. With the standard Fano convention $e_1 e_2 = e_4$, the full multiplication table determines all products. The key structural fact is that $J^2 = -\mathrm{Id}$ holds by alternativity regardless of the specific labeling.)*

This gives $W \cong \mathbb{C}^3$ as a complex vector space (with $J$ playing the role of multiplication by $i$), so:
$$\mathbb{O} = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{C}^3 \qquad \text{(as real vector spaces: } 2 + 6 = 8\text{)}$$

The three complex coordinates of $\mathbb{C}^3$ are the three pairs of real directions related by $J$:
$$(e_k, J(e_k)) = (e_k, e_7 \cdot e_k), \qquad k = 1, 2, 3$$

where $e_7 e_1, e_7 e_2, e_7 e_3$ give the three "imaginary parts" paired with $e_1, e_2, e_3$ respectively.

**Dimension verification:** $\dim_{\mathbb{R}}(\mathbb{O}) = \dim_{\mathbb{R}}(\mathbb{C}) + \dim_{\mathbb{R}}(\mathbb{C}^3) = 2 + 6 = 8$. Correct.

**Freedom in the construction:** The choice $u = e_7$ is one point on $S^6$. A different choice of $u$ gives a different splitting $\mathbb{O} = \mathbb{C}' \oplus (\mathbb{C}')^3$ and a different Cl(6) subalgebra. The space of such choices is $S^6 = G_2/\mathrm{SU}(3)$, where $G_2 = \mathrm{Aut}(\mathbb{O})$ and $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ is the stabilizer of $u$ in $G_2$. This $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ is precisely the automorphism group of $\mathbb{C}^3$ preserving the complex structure $J$ --- it will be identified with the color group $\mathrm{SU}(3)_C$.

### Step 2: From Im(O) Directions to Cl(10) Generators

Phase 18 established $\mathrm{Spin}(10)$ acting on $S_{10}^+$ (the 16-dim complex Weyl spinor). The Lie algebra $\mathfrak{spin}(10)$ has the vector representation on $\mathbb{R}^{10}$. The full Clifford algebra $\mathrm{Cl}(10)$ is generated by 10 elements $\Gamma_1, \ldots, \Gamma_{10}$ satisfying:
$$\{\Gamma_A, \Gamma_B\} = 2\delta_{AB}, \qquad A, B = 1, \ldots, 10$$

The 10 directions correspond to the 10-dim representation $V_0^{\mathbb{C}} = \mathbf{10}$ of $\mathrm{Spin}(10)$ established in Phase 18. However, for the internal Cl(6), we do not need the full 10 generators --- only those corresponding to the 6 directions of $W = \mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{C}^3)$ inside $\mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{O})$.

Following Todorov (arXiv:2206.06912): the 7 imaginary octonion directions $e_1, \ldots, e_7$ give 7 of the generators of $\mathrm{Cl}(10)$ (the "internal" directions). The remaining 3 generators come from additional structure in the $V_0$ Peirce space (corresponding to the $h_2(\mathbb{O})$ sector).

For the Cl(6) construction, we identify 6 generators:
$$\gamma_k := \Gamma_k, \qquad k = 1, \ldots, 6$$

corresponding to the 6 directions $e_1, \ldots, e_6 \in W = e_7^{\perp} \cap \mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{O})$. These inherit the Clifford relations from $\mathrm{Cl}(10)$:
$$\{\gamma_i, \gamma_j\} = 2\delta_{ij}, \qquad i, j = 1, \ldots, 6$$

and generate a $\mathrm{Cl}(6)$ subalgebra:
$$\mathrm{Cl}(6) = \mathrm{Alg}(\gamma_1, \ldots, \gamma_6) \subset \mathrm{Cl}(10)$$

**Key point:** The $\mathrm{Cl}(6)$ subalgebra is not ad hoc --- it is *induced* by the choice of complex structure $u = e_7$ on $\mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{O})$. The 6 generators come from the $\mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{C}^3)$ directions, and the excluded directions are $e_7$ (the complex structure itself) plus the 3 non-octonion generators of $\mathrm{Cl}(10)$.

### Step 3: The Volume Form $\omega_6$

Define the volume element of $\mathrm{Cl}(6)$:
$$\omega_6 = \gamma_1 \gamma_2 \gamma_3 \gamma_4 \gamma_5 \gamma_6$$

**Property (a): $\omega_6^2$.**

We compute $\omega_6^2 = (\gamma_1 \gamma_2 \gamma_3 \gamma_4 \gamma_5 \gamma_6)^2$.

To evaluate this, move $\gamma_6$ (the rightmost factor of the first copy) past $\gamma_1, \gamma_2, \gamma_3, \gamma_4, \gamma_5$ of the second copy. Each anticommutation past a different generator costs a factor of $(-1)$:
- $\gamma_6$ past $\gamma_1$: factor $(-1)$
- $\gamma_6$ past $\gamma_2$: factor $(-1)$
- $\gamma_6$ past $\gamma_3$: factor $(-1)$
- $\gamma_6$ past $\gamma_4$: factor $(-1)$
- $\gamma_6$ past $\gamma_5$: factor $(-1)$
- Then $\gamma_6 \gamma_6 = 1$.

Total: 5 sign flips for $\gamma_6$. Similarly, $\gamma_5$ must pass 4 remaining generators, giving 4 sign flips. Continuing:

Total number of transpositions $= 5 + 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 + 0 = 15 = \frac{6 \times 5}{2}$.

Each transposition contributes $(-1)$, and each $\gamma_k^2 = 1$, so:
$$\omega_6^2 = (-1)^{15} \cdot 1 = -1$$

Equivalently, by the general formula for the Clifford volume element in $\mathrm{Cl}(n)$ with positive-definite signature:
$$\omega_n^2 = (-1)^{n(n-1)/2}$$

For $n = 6$: $(-1)^{6 \cdot 5/2} = (-1)^{15} = -1$. Confirmed.

**Property (b): The chirality operator $i\omega_6$.**

Define the chirality operator $\Gamma_{\mathrm{chir}} = i\omega_6$. Then:
$$(i\omega_6)^2 = i^2 \cdot \omega_6^2 = (-1)(-1) = +1$$

So $i\omega_6$ is an involution (squares to the identity).

**Property (c): The projector $P$.**

Define:
$$P = \frac{1}{2}(1 - i\omega_6)$$

This is an idempotent:
$$P^2 = \frac{1}{4}(1 - i\omega_6)^2 = \frac{1}{4}(1 - 2i\omega_6 + (i\omega_6)^2) = \frac{1}{4}(1 - 2i\omega_6 + 1) = \frac{1}{2}(1 - i\omega_6) = P$$

### Step 4: Action of $P$ on the 32-dim Dirac Spinor

The Dirac spinor of $\mathrm{Spin}(10)$ is $\Delta_{10} = S_{10}^+ \oplus S_{10}^-$, with $\dim(\Delta_{10}) = 32$. Under the $\mathrm{Cl}(10)$ chirality operator:
$$\Gamma_{11} = \Gamma_1 \Gamma_2 \cdots \Gamma_{10}$$
$S_{10}^+$ has eigenvalue $+1$ and $S_{10}^-$ has eigenvalue $-1$ (or vice versa, depending on convention; this choice is the chirality convention).

The $\mathrm{Cl}(6)$ volume form $\omega_6 = \gamma_1 \cdots \gamma_6$ is *distinct* from $\Gamma_{11}$. They are related by:
$$\Gamma_{11} = \omega_6 \cdot \Gamma_7 \Gamma_8 \Gamma_9 \Gamma_{10}$$

Since $\omega_6^2 = -1$, its eigenvalues on $\Delta_{10}$ are $\pm i$, each with multiplicity 16. The projector $P = \frac{1}{2}(1 - i\omega_6)$ selects the eigenspace with eigenvalue $-i$ (called the "particle" eigenspace following Todorov 2022).

**Trace computation:**
$$\mathrm{tr}(P) = \frac{1}{2}\left(\mathrm{tr}(\mathbb{1}_{32}) - i \cdot \mathrm{tr}(\omega_6)\right)$$

Since $\omega_6 = \gamma_1 \cdots \gamma_6$ is a product of 6 anticommuting generators, its trace on any representation is zero (it maps every basis vector to an orthogonal vector --- formally, $\omega_6$ anticommutes with each $\gamma_k$, so any diagonal matrix element vanishes).

More precisely: $\omega_6$ anticommutes with each $\gamma_k$ individually (since $\gamma_k \omega_6 = (-1)^5 \omega_6 \gamma_k = -\omega_6 \gamma_k$ for each $k \in \{1,...,6\}$). Therefore, if $|v\rangle$ is an eigenvector of $\omega_6$ with eigenvalue $\lambda$, then $\gamma_k |v\rangle$ is an eigenvector with eigenvalue $-\lambda$. The eigenvalues come in $\pm$ pairs, so $\mathrm{tr}(\omega_6) = 0$ on any module where all $\gamma_k$ act.

On the 32-dim Dirac spinor $\Delta_{10}$ of $\mathrm{Cl}(10)$, the $\gamma_k$ for $k=1,...,6$ certainly act (as a subset of the $\mathrm{Cl}(10)$ generators). Therefore:
$$\mathrm{tr}(\omega_6)\big|_{\Delta_{10}} = 0$$

and:
$$\mathrm{tr}(P) = \frac{1}{2}(32 - 0) = 16$$

**Result:** $P$ selects a 16-dimensional subspace of the 32-dim Dirac spinor. This 16-dim subspace corresponds to one generation of Standard Model fermions (Todorov 2022).

3. CONVENTION CHECK: Euclidean positive-definite Clifford convention ({gamma_i, gamma_j} = 2 delta_ij, gamma_i^2 = +1). Consistent throughout.

## Part II: Pati-Salam Breaking Chain and Furey Witt Decomposition

### Step 5: Pati-Salam Subgroup from $\omega_6$

The volume form $\omega_6$ breaks $\mathrm{Spin}(10)$ to its stabilizer subgroup. The 10 generators of $\mathrm{Cl}(10)$ split as:
- **6 internal generators:** $\gamma_1, \ldots, \gamma_6$ from $\mathrm{Cl}(6)$ (the $\mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{C}^3)$ directions)
- **4 external generators:** $\Gamma_7, \Gamma_8, \Gamma_9, \Gamma_{10}$ (the complement: $u = e_7$ direction plus 3 additional)

The Lie algebra $\mathfrak{spin}(10)$ is spanned by the bivectors:
$$\left\{\frac{1}{4}[\Gamma_A, \Gamma_B] = \frac{1}{2}\Gamma_A \Gamma_B : A < B\right\}$$

with $\dim(\mathfrak{spin}(10)) = \binom{10}{2} = 45$.

To find the stabilizer of $\omega_6$, we need those bivectors that commute with $\omega_6$.

**Key commutation property:** Each individual generator $\gamma_k$ (for $k = 1, \ldots, 6$) anticommutes with $\omega_6$:
$$\gamma_k \omega_6 = \gamma_k (\gamma_1 \cdots \gamma_6) = (-1)^5 \omega_6 \gamma_k = -\omega_6 \gamma_k$$

since $\gamma_k$ must anticommute past the other 5 generators in $\omega_6$ (and commutes with itself, producing $\gamma_k^2 = 1$ at its position, but this is more carefully seen as: $\gamma_k$ commutes past itself trivially and anticommutes past the other 5, giving $(-1)^5$).

Similarly, each external generator $\Gamma_A$ (for $A = 7, 8, 9, 10$) commutes with $\omega_6$:
$$\Gamma_A \omega_6 = \omega_6 \Gamma_A$$
since $\Gamma_A$ anticommutes with each $\gamma_k$ individually (they are distinct $\mathrm{Cl}(10)$ generators), and $\omega_6$ contains 6 factors of $\gamma_k$, so:
$$\Gamma_A \omega_6 = (-1)^6 \omega_6 \Gamma_A = \omega_6 \Gamma_A$$

Now consider the three types of bivectors:

**(i) Internal-internal:** $\gamma_i \gamma_j$ for $i, j \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}$, $i < j$.

$$(\gamma_i \gamma_j) \omega_6 = \gamma_i (\gamma_j \omega_6) = \gamma_i (-\omega_6 \gamma_j) = -(-\omega_6 \gamma_i)\gamma_j = \omega_6 \gamma_i \gamma_j = \omega_6 (\gamma_i \gamma_j)$$

So $[\gamma_i \gamma_j, \omega_6] = 0$. These generate $\mathfrak{spin}(6) \cong \mathfrak{su}(4)$.

Count: $\binom{6}{2} = 15$. Dimension of $\mathfrak{su}(4) = 15$. Consistent.

**(ii) External-external:** $\Gamma_A \Gamma_B$ for $A, B \in \{7, 8, 9, 10\}$, $A < B$.

$$(\Gamma_A \Gamma_B) \omega_6 = \Gamma_A (\Gamma_B \omega_6) = \Gamma_A (\omega_6 \Gamma_B) = (\Gamma_A \omega_6) \Gamma_B = \omega_6 \Gamma_A \Gamma_B = \omega_6 (\Gamma_A \Gamma_B)$$

So $[\Gamma_A \Gamma_B, \omega_6] = 0$. These generate $\mathfrak{spin}(4) \cong \mathfrak{su}(2)_L \oplus \mathfrak{su}(2)_R$.

Count: $\binom{4}{2} = 6$. Dimension of $\mathfrak{su}(2)_L + \mathfrak{su}(2)_R = 3 + 3 = 6$. Consistent.

**(iii) Mixed:** $\gamma_i \Gamma_A$ for $i \in \{1, \ldots, 6\}$, $A \in \{7, \ldots, 10\}$.

$$(\gamma_i \Gamma_A) \omega_6 = \gamma_i (\Gamma_A \omega_6) = \gamma_i (\omega_6 \Gamma_A) = (\gamma_i \omega_6) \Gamma_A = (-\omega_6 \gamma_i) \Gamma_A = -\omega_6 (\gamma_i \Gamma_A)$$

So $[\gamma_i \Gamma_A, \omega_6] \neq 0$ (they anticommute with $\omega_6$). These are NOT in the stabilizer.

Count: $6 \times 4 = 24$.

**Total:** Stabilizer dimension = $15 + 6 = 21$. Complement dimension = $24$. Check: $21 + 24 = 45 = \dim(\mathfrak{spin}(10))$. Correct.

**Group-level identification:**

$$\mathrm{Stab}_{\mathrm{Spin}(10)}(\omega_6) = \frac{\mathrm{Spin}(6) \times \mathrm{Spin}(4)}{\mathbb{Z}_2}$$

Using the exceptional isomorphisms:
- $\mathrm{Spin}(6) \cong \mathrm{SU}(4)$ (the Pati-Salam color-lepton group)
- $\mathrm{Spin}(4) \cong \mathrm{SU}(2)_L \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_R$ (the left-right symmetric weak group)

So:
$$\mathrm{Stab}_{\mathrm{Spin}(10)}(\omega_6) \cong \frac{\mathrm{SU}(4) \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_L \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_R}{\mathbb{Z}_2}$$

This is the **Pati-Salam group** (Todorov 2022).

### Step 6: Decomposition of the 16 under Pati-Salam

The 16-dim Weyl spinor $S_{10}^+$ of $\mathrm{Spin}(10)$ (one generation of fermions, established in Phase 18 as $V_{1/2}^{\mathbb{C}}$) decomposes under $\mathrm{SU}(4) \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_L \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_R$ as:

$$\mathbf{16} \to (\mathbf{4}, \mathbf{2}, \mathbf{1}) \oplus (\bar{\mathbf{4}}, \mathbf{1}, \mathbf{2})$$

**Dimension check:** $4 \times 2 \times 1 + 4 \times 1 \times 2 = 8 + 8 = 16$. Correct.

**Physical interpretation:**
- $(\mathbf{4}, \mathbf{2}, \mathbf{1})$: 4 colors (3 quark colors + 1 lepton) $\times$ SU(2)$_L$ doublet $\times$ SU(2)$_R$ singlet = **left-handed fermions**
  - Content: $(u_L, d_L)$ in 3 colors + $(\nu_L, e_L)$
- $(\bar{\mathbf{4}}, \mathbf{1}, \mathbf{2})$: $\bar{4}$ colors $\times$ SU(2)$_L$ singlet $\times$ SU(2)$_R$ doublet = **right-handed fermions**
  - Content: $(u_R, d_R)$ in 3 colors + $(\nu_R, e_R)$

**This is the LEFT (chiral) embedding:** SU(2)$_L$ acts ONLY on the $(\mathbf{4}, \mathbf{2}, \mathbf{1})$ component --- the left-handed fermions. Right-handed fermions are SU(2)$_L$ singlets. This is precisely the Standard Model chirality pattern.

**Distinction from the diagonal embedding (Baez-Sawin):** In the diagonal embedding, a single SU(2) would act on BOTH chiralities symmetrically. That would give a non-chiral theory, which is NOT the Standard Model. The key theorem of Sawin (cited in Baez, n-Category Cafe Part 13) establishes that the Spin(10) GUT embedding of the SM is the LEFT embedding, not the diagonal. Our construction reproduces this: the volume form $\omega_6$ separates left and right sectors before the Pati-Salam breaking, ensuring SU(2)$_L$ acts only on the left-handed component.

### Step 7: Breaking SU(4) $\to$ SU(3) $\times$ U(1)

The complex structure $u$ distinguishes $\mathbb{C}$ (the lepton direction, associated with $u$ itself) from $\mathbb{C}^3$ (the quark/color directions):

$$\mathbb{C}^4 = \mathbb{C}_{\text{lepton}} \oplus \mathbb{C}^3_{\text{color}}$$

The stabilizer of this decomposition in SU(4) is:
$$\mathrm{SU}(3) \times \mathrm{U}(1) \subset \mathrm{SU}(4)$$

where SU(3) acts on $\mathbb{C}^3_{\text{color}}$ (= the color group SU(3)$_C$) and U(1) is the relative phase between the lepton and color sectors.

After this breaking, the Standard Model gauge group emerges:
$$\mathrm{SU}(3)_C \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_L \times \mathrm{U}(1)_Y$$

where the hypercharge U(1)$_Y$ is a specific combination of the U(1) from $\mathrm{SU}(4) \to \mathrm{SU}(3) \times \mathrm{U}(1)$ and the Cartan subalgebra of SU(2)$_R$.

**Dimension check:** $\dim(\mathrm{SU}(3)_C) + \dim(\mathrm{SU}(2)_L) + \dim(\mathrm{U}(1)_Y) = 8 + 3 + 1 = 12$. Consistent with the SM.

**Full breaking chain:**

Both breaking steps are driven by the same input: the choice of complex structure $u = e_7$ on $\mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{O})$.

### Step 8: Furey Witt Decomposition

Following Furey (arXiv:1806.00612), we construct creation and annihilation operators from the $\mathrm{Cl}(6)$ generators.

**Witt basis construction:** Pair the 6 generators into 3 creation and 3 annihilation operators:
$$a_j = \frac{1}{2}(\gamma_{2j-1} + i\gamma_{2j}), \qquad a_j^{\dagger} = \frac{1}{2}(\gamma_{2j-1} - i\gamma_{2j}), \qquad j = 1, 2, 3$$

In Furey's octonion language, this becomes:
$$a_j = \frac{1}{2}(-e_{j+3} + e_7 \cdot e_j) = \frac{1}{2}(-e_{j+3} + J(e_j)), \qquad j = 1, 2, 3$$

where the complex structure $J(e_j) = e_7 \cdot e_j$ plays the role of the factor $i$ in the Witt construction (connecting to the convention $a_j = (1/2)(-e_{j+4} + i \cdot e_j)$ used in the plan, with appropriate Fano-plane index remapping).

**Canonical anticommutation relations (CAR):**

We verify all three sets of relations:

**(i)** $\{a_i, a_j^{\dagger}\} = \delta_{ij}$:

$$\{a_i, a_j^{\dagger}\} = \frac{1}{4}\{(\gamma_{2i-1} + i\gamma_{2i}), (\gamma_{2j-1} - i\gamma_{2j})\}$$

$$= \frac{1}{4}\left(\{\gamma_{2i-1}, \gamma_{2j-1}\} - i\{\gamma_{2i-1}, \gamma_{2j}\} + i\{\gamma_{2i}, \gamma_{2j-1}\} + \{\gamma_{2i}, \gamma_{2j}\}\right)$$

$$= \frac{1}{4}\left(2\delta_{2i-1,2j-1} - i \cdot 2\delta_{2i-1,2j} + i \cdot 2\delta_{2i,2j-1} + 2\delta_{2i,2j}\right)$$

For $i = j$: The middle two terms vanish ($2i-1 \neq 2i$), giving:
$$\frac{1}{4}(2 + 2) = 1 = \delta_{ii}$$

For $i \neq j$: All Kronecker deltas are zero (since $2i-1 \neq 2j-1$, $2i-1 \neq 2j$, $2i \neq 2j-1$, $2i \neq 2j$ when $i \neq j$), giving $0 = \delta_{ij}$. Correct.

**(ii)** $\{a_i, a_j\} = 0$:

$$\{a_i, a_j\} = \frac{1}{4}\{(\gamma_{2i-1} + i\gamma_{2i}), (\gamma_{2j-1} + i\gamma_{2j})\}$$

$$= \frac{1}{4}\left(\{\gamma_{2i-1}, \gamma_{2j-1}\} + i\{\gamma_{2i-1}, \gamma_{2j}\} + i\{\gamma_{2i}, \gamma_{2j-1}\} - \{\gamma_{2i}, \gamma_{2j}\}\right)$$

$$= \frac{1}{4}\left(2\delta_{2i-1,2j-1} + 2i\delta_{2i-1,2j} + 2i\delta_{2i,2j-1} - 2\delta_{2i,2j}\right)$$

For $i = j$: $\frac{1}{4}(2 + 0 + 0 - 2) = 0$. Correct.
For $i \neq j$: All terms zero. Correct.

**(iii)** $\{a_i^{\dagger}, a_j^{\dagger}\} = 0$: Identical argument with $i \to -i$. Gives 0. Correct.

All 27 anticommutation relations verified.

This is the **Witt decomposition** of $\mathbb{C}^6 = W^3 \oplus (\bar{W})^3$, where $W$ is the maximally isotropic (null) subspace spanned by the $a_j$, and $\bar{W}$ is spanned by the $a_j^{\dagger}$.

### Step 9: Automatic Chirality from the Witt Decomposition

The Clifford vacuum $|0\rangle$ is defined by:
$$a_j |0\rangle = 0, \qquad j = 1, 2, 3$$

The $2^3 = 8$ states built by creation operators are:

| State | Particle number |
|-------|----------------|
| $\|0\rangle$ | 0 |
| $a_i^{\dagger}\|0\rangle$ | 1 (three states) |
| $a_i^{\dagger} a_j^{\dagger}\|0\rangle$ | 2 (three states, $i < j$) |
| $a_1^{\dagger} a_2^{\dagger} a_3^{\dagger}\|0\rangle$ | 3 (one state) |

Total: $1 + 3 + 3 + 1 = 8$ states. These form one chirality sector.

**The chirality eigenvalue of $\omega_6$ on these states:**

The volume form acts on the Clifford vacuum as:
$$\omega_6 |0\rangle = \gamma_1 \gamma_2 \gamma_3 \gamma_4 \gamma_5 \gamma_6 |0\rangle$$

Using $\gamma_{2j-1} = a_j + a_j^{\dagger}$ and $\gamma_{2j} = -i(a_j - a_j^{\dagger})$:

$$\omega_6 = (\gamma_1 \gamma_2)(\gamma_3 \gamma_4)(\gamma_5 \gamma_6)$$

For each pair:
$$\gamma_{2j-1}\gamma_{2j} = (a_j + a_j^{\dagger})(-i)(a_j - a_j^{\dagger}) = -i(a_j + a_j^{\dagger})(a_j - a_j^{\dagger})$$
$$= -i(a_j a_j - a_j a_j^{\dagger} + a_j^{\dagger} a_j - a_j^{\dagger} a_j^{\dagger})$$
$$= -i(0 - (1 - a_j^{\dagger} a_j) + a_j^{\dagger} a_j - 0) = -i(-1 + 2a_j^{\dagger} a_j) = i(1 - 2a_j^{\dagger} a_j)$$

So $\gamma_{2j-1}\gamma_{2j} = i(1 - 2n_j)$ where $n_j = a_j^{\dagger} a_j$ is the number operator.

Therefore:
$$\omega_6 = \prod_{j=1}^{3} i(1 - 2n_j) = i^3 \prod_{j=1}^{3}(1 - 2n_j) = -i \prod_{j=1}^{3}(-1)^{n_j}$$

$$\omega_6 = -i \cdot (-1)^{n_1 + n_2 + n_3} = -i \cdot (-1)^N$$

where $N = n_1 + n_2 + n_3$ is the total particle number.

**Eigenvalues:**
- Even particle number ($N = 0, 2$): $\omega_6 = -i \cdot (+1) = -i$
- Odd particle number ($N = 1, 3$): $\omega_6 = -i \cdot (-1) = +i$

So the 8 "particle" states split by parity:
- $N$ even: $|0\rangle$, $a_i^{\dagger} a_j^{\dagger}|0\rangle$ (4 states) $\longrightarrow$ eigenvalue $-i$
- $N$ odd: $a_i^{\dagger}|0\rangle$, $a_1^{\dagger} a_2^{\dagger} a_3^{\dagger}|0\rangle$ (4 states) $\longrightarrow$ eigenvalue $+i$

The SU(2) that arises from the Witt decomposition acts via bilinears in the creation and annihilation operators.
