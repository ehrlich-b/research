\section{Area-Law Entanglement}
\label{sec:arealaw}

The Jacobson entanglement equilibrium argument~\cite{Jacobson2016}
requires two inputs from the UV theory: (J1)~the entanglement entropy
variation $\delta\ent$ scales with the boundary area, not the volume;
and (J2)~the entanglement first law
$\delta\ent = \delta\langle\modH\rangle$ holds exactly.  We now
establish both for the self-modeling lattice.

\subsection{Entanglement first law}
\label{sec:first-law}

For any quantum state $\rho$ with reduced state
$\rhoA = \Tr_B(\rho)$ and modular Hamiltonian
$\modH = -\ln \rhoA$, the first-order change in the von~Neumann
entropy under a perturbation $\rho \to \rho + \delta\rho$ with
$\Tr(\delta\rho) = 0$ is
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\delta \ent(A) = \delta\langle \modH \rangle.}
  \label{eq:first-law}
\end{equation}
This is an \emph{exact identity} of quantum information
theory~\cite{BCHM2013, LMVR2014}: expanding
$\ent = -\Tr(\rhoA \ln \rhoA)$ to first order in $\delta\rhoA$ and
using $\Tr(\delta\rhoA) = 0$ gives
$\delta\ent = -\Tr(\delta\rhoA \ln\rhoA) = \Tr(\delta\rhoA \, \modH)
= \delta\langle\modH\rangle$.  No approximations or additional
assumptions are involved.  This establishes Jacobson
input~(J2).

\subsection{Area-law entanglement bounds}
\label{sec:area-bounds}

Consider a bipartition of the lattice $V = A \cup B$ with boundary
$\partial A = \{\langle x,y \rangle \in E : x \in A,\, y \in B\}$ of
size $|\partial A|$.  We establish area-law bounds from three
complementary perspectives, covering different state classes and
requiring different assumptions.

\subsubsection{Thermal mutual information (WVCH)}
\label{sec:wvch}

\emph{Assumption:}  The state is a Gibbs state
$\rho_\beta = e^{-\beta H}/Z$ at inverse temperature
$\beta = 1/T > 0$.

For any Gibbs state of a local Hamiltonian, the quantum mutual
information $I(A{:}B) = \ent(A) + \ent(B) - \ent(AB)$ satisfies
the Wolf--Verstraete--Cirac--Hastings (WVCH)
bound~\cite{WVCH2008}:
\begin{equation}
  I(A{:}B) \leq 2\beta \sum_{\substack{X :\, X \cap A \neq \emptyset \\
  X \cap B \neq \emptyset}} \|\Phi(X)\|.
  \label{eq:wvch-general}
\end{equation}
For the self-modeling Hamiltonian~\eqref{eq:hamiltonian} with
nearest-neighbor coupling $\|h_{xy}\| = |J|$, this reduces to
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{I(A{:}B) \leq 2\beta\, |\partial A|\, |J|.}
  \label{eq:wvch-bound}
\end{equation}
The mutual information scales with the boundary area $|\partial A|$,
not the volume~$|A|$.  The bound holds in all spatial
dimensions, requires no spectral gap, and depends on $|J|$ (not
$\mathrm{sign}(J)$), so it applies equally to the AFM and FM cases.

\subsubsection{Pure-state entanglement entropy (channel capacity)}
\label{sec:channel-capacity}

\emph{Assumption:}  The global state is a pure state
$|\psi\rangle \in \mathcal{H}_A \otimes \mathcal{H}_B$.

Self-modeling locality constrains all correlations between $A$ and $B$
to be mediated by the $|\partial A|$ boundary bonds.  Each bond
connects two $n$-dimensional systems.  By the Schmidt decomposition,
the entanglement across a single bond is at most $\log n$~nats (the
maximum von~Neumann entropy of one factor of $\mathbb{C}^n \otimes
\mathbb{C}^n$).  By subadditivity, the total entanglement entropy
contributed by $|\partial A|$ independent bonds satisfies
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\ent(A) \leq \log(n) \cdot |\partial A|.}
  \label{eq:channel-bound}
\end{equation}
This is an area law for the von~Neumann entropy: it scales with the
boundary, not the volume.  The bound depends only on the graph
structure~$G$ and the local dimension~$n$, not on the
Hamiltonian~$h_{xy}$, the coupling~$J$, or the temperature.  The
coefficient $\log n$ is the maximum entanglement a single bond can
carry, achieved by a maximally entangled state.  (In the language of
quantum communication, $\log n$ is the entanglement capacity of one
bond~\cite{Holevo1973, BennettWiesner1992}; the bound above follows
from the simpler Schmidt/subadditivity argument.)

\subsubsection{Perturbative $\delta\ent$ (modular Hamiltonian locality)}
\label{sec:delta-s}

The Jacobson argument requires not that $\ent(A)$ itself obeys an area
law, but that the \emph{variation} $\delta\ent$ under small
perturbations scales with $|\partial A|$.  From the entanglement first
law~\eqref{eq:first-law},
\begin{equation}
  \delta\ent = \delta\langle\modH\rangle.
  \label{eq:delta-s-modH}
\end{equation}
If the modular Hamiltonian $\modH = -\ln\rhoA$ is concentrated near
the boundary~$\partial A$ (i.e., its matrix elements decay rapidly
with distance from the boundary), then
$\delta\langle\modH\rangle \sim O(|\partial A|)$ for local
perturbations, giving
\begin{equation}
  \delta\ent \sim O(|\partial A|).
  \label{eq:delta-s-boundary}
\end{equation}

Modular Hamiltonian locality is physically motivated by the
Bisognano--Wichmann theorem~\cite{BisognanoWichmann1976} (the modular
Hamiltonian of the vacuum in a Rindler wedge is the boost generator,
localized at the entangling surface) and by Peschel's result for free
lattice fermions~\cite{Peschel2003} ($\modH$ is a nearest-neighbor
hopping operator on the entangling surface).  For the self-modeling
Hamiltonian, numerical evidence (Sec.~\ref{sec:numerical}) supports
this: the short-range fraction of $\modH$ is $0.9993$ for the
antiferromagnetic Heisenberg chain at $N = 16$.

This perspective is the weakest of the three, since modular
Hamiltonian locality is not proven in general for the self-modeling
lattice.  However, it is the most directly relevant for the Jacobson
argument: it establishes that $\delta\ent$ is an area-law quantity even
when $\ent(A)$ itself has logarithmic corrections (as in the gapless
AFM case).

\subsection{What this establishes}
\label{sec:area-summary}

The three perspectives complement each other:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Thermal states:} $I(A{:}B) \leq 2\beta|J|\,|\partial A|$
  (WVCH, no gap required).
\item \textbf{Pure states:} $\ent(A) \leq \log(n)\,|\partial A|$
  (channel capacity, Hamiltonian-independent).
\item \textbf{Any state, perturbatively:}
  $\delta\ent \sim O(|\partial A|)$ (modular Hamiltonian locality).
\end{itemize}
Together, they establish Jacobson input~(J1): the entanglement entropy
variation $\delta\ent$ scales with the boundary area, not the volume.
Combined with the entanglement first law~\eqref{eq:first-law}, which
establishes Jacobson input~(J2), the self-modeling lattice provides the
two UV inputs required by Jacobson's entanglement equilibrium
argument.

\emph{Note on Hastings (2007).}---For a comprehensive review of area
laws in quantum systems, see Ref.~\cite{EisertCramerPlenio2010}.  The
Hastings area-law theorem~\cite{Hastings2007} for one-dimensional
gapped ground states
does not directly apply here: the self-modeling Hamiltonian is gapless
for both signs of~$J$ in one dimension (the AFM Heisenberg chain
flows to the $SU(2)_1$ WZW conformal field theory with central
charge~$c = 1$; the FM ground state is gapped only above the
degenerate ground-state manifold, with gap $\sim O(1/N^2) \to 0$).
We cite Hastings for historical context; our area-law arguments via
WVCH and channel capacity do not require a spectral gap.
